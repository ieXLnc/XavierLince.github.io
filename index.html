About me

From a neurosciences background, I have extensive experience in research project management, I am creative and concrete problem-solving oriented.

I am familiar with complex neurophysiological datasets, during my master's, I realised my thesis on memorisation techniques at the @NEAD working with EEG analysis. I also worked at @Connectomics where we explored the role of the school environment on children and its impact on emotional regulation with MRI functional connectivity methods. Recently I worked at the @HEP Valais on how to digitalise creativity tools.

Passionate about coding, I did the @EPFL extension school where I learned to work with the entire data pipeline, from data analysis with Pandas, NumPy or Matplotlib to complex machine learning and deep learning models with Scikit-learn, TensorFlow, and Keras. This extension school allowed me to have an advanced understanding of programming in Python with more than 450h of coding.

I would now like to direct my career in the data analysis and machine learning field. I am currently working on a project in collaboration with the University of Lausanne where I create state of the art DRL agents to assess the quality of their learning based on the type of feedback given to them. My interest in coding does not stop in neurosciences but extend to a wide range of datasets that I found relevant. I am keen on making a difference with the code I make and aim at using that code to bring concrete changes and relevant conclusions in the world of today.
